Campaign Overview
The Challenge
Increase usage of the 988 Helpline by growing awareness, promoting the confidentiality of resources, and ensuring all Baltimoreans know where to start
Team Aptive's Solution
Design a multi-channel outreach campaign that is designed by Baltimore, for Baltimore.
Focus broad messaging to dispel the myth that a resident's problems aren't "big enough" and convert hesitation to action
Develop tailored campaigns for specific neighborhoods, populations, and situations
Creative Approach
Ground all visuals and messages in research
Use colors, language, and typography that feels warm, encouraging, and authentic
Include messaging to emphasize that 988 is available to everyone and is confidential and trustworthy
Use supporting design elements (shapes, gradients, imagery) to engender feelings of hope, trust, and support

The Big Picture

All it takes is hello. That's it.

Our concept is tied to research that suggests the people who need 988 most are often the people who don't think they qualify. Whether someone is experiencing a crisis or just carrying something they haven't told anyone, this campaign works to lower the stakes of just reaching out for help. Call, text, or chat — you don't have to have the right words, just say hello.

988 is here for all of it. For anxiety, grief, and hard days. For people experiencing a mental health emergency. For someone worried about a neighbor. For a parent scared for their kid. For a friend who doesn't know what else to do. Just start with "Hello."

Grounded in Research

Lowering the stakes. BHSB's research shows that people don't call because they don't think their problem is big enough. This campaign highlights that no problem is too small — or too big — for 988 counselors.

No clinical language. Everyday words like "anxious," "grieving," and "overwhelmed" make 988 feel accessible to everyone.

Always confidential. Across demographics surveyed, fear of being found out was a primary barrier. By emphasizing confidentiality, we elevate that concern immediately.

Human, not institutional. Distrust of systems is heightened across Baltimore, particularly in communities already navigating other pressures. Our ads position 988 as a trusted resource, not a government program.

Hopeful, not heavy. Research showed that despairing visuals cause people to disengage. We choose our colors and images to highlight warmth and trust.

Priority Audiences
Youth Ages 13 to 24

We tailor creative for this audience to focus on text and chat as primary entry points. Sports figures and trusted community voices serve as messengers.

Men 45 and Older, Including Veterans

We focus on practical, stigma-reducing messages for men, with the phone number as the primary entry point.

LGBTQ+ Residents

Messaging will center affirming, culturally competent language. The dedicated 988 LGBTQ+ youth line is available 24/7 and featured prominently for this audience.

People in or Approaching a Mental Health Emergency

988 counselors are equipped to handle even the most serious of crises — we remind audiences that you should never hesitate to call when it matters most.

Audience priorities and messaging approaches will be refined through community sessions and additional research prior to final creative development. Emerging community needs — including populations navigating heightened stress due to current events — will be incorporated as the campaign evolves.

Alternate Concepts

These approaches address the same brief differently, each still informed by the research. Whichever direction moves forward will ultimately be pressure-tested through focus groups and community input before final development.

Three executions, one platform. Don't Wait. Call. 988. / Don't Wait. Text. 988. / Don't Wait. Chat. 988. Each version targets a different entry point.

There is an opportunity for this concept to be a jingle or memorable audio. BGE's downed wire campaign is a useful reference — a serious public safety message that stuck with people because it was set to music.

Grounded in BHSB's finding that people don't call because they don't think their problem is big enough. Strongest for audiences who need a push more than a permission slip.

Trusted community voices asking the question outperform institutional messaging. Strongest for social and community activation channels.
